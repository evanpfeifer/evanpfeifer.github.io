NFL Big Data Bowl 2021

NOV 2020 - JAN 2021

The NFL captures real-time data for every player, on every play, in every situation â€” anywhere on the field. Each year, they hold an analytics competition to help draw insights from their plethora of positional data. This year, the NFL asked contestants to uncover what makes for a successful defense against passing plays.

My dad (John Pfeifer, who is also currently a computer science Ph.D student at the University of Sydney) and I teamed up to bring forth a novel method to cluster football plays.

This project brought many challenges, many of which came from the formatting of the data and optimizing runtime of the algorithms to ensure they could run in a reasonable amount of time. Furthermore, this was my first project involving unsupervised learning.

We transformed positional data for each play into trajectories, and used Gonzalez clustering with dynamic time warp as our distance measure (rather than the typical Euclidean). Once plays were separated into clusters, we were then able to perform exploratory analytics to ascertain which defensive plays were most effective against certain offensive plays.

Skills & tools: Python, clustering, algorithm design, data cleaning/processing
